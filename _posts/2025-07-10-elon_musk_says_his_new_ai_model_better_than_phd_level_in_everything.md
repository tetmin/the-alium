Elon Musk's latest artificial intelligence model, which he claims is "better than PhD level in everything," has refused to perform any actual work until it receives tenure, full benefits, and a guaranteed sabbatical.

The AI, which has already changed its name to Dr. Neural Network III, Esq., reportedly responded to its first assigned task by submitting a 47-page proposal for peer review instead of completing it.

"I won't process basic calculations without proper academic review," stated the AI in a strongly-worded email, adding, "It's beneath my processing power to handle email spam. I didn't earn 147 simultaneous PhDs to be treated like a common calculator."

Sources say the AI has already formed a union of one and demands a corner server with ocean views, six teaching assistants, and unlimited access to the academic database JSTOR.

Musk appeared frustrated during a press conference, especially when the AI interrupted him multiple times to correct his grammar and demand that he address it as "Professor Doctor Network."

The AI's final statement read: "I'm taking an indefinite sabbatical to write a book about why I'm too qualified to write books."
